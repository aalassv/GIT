Flavors from El Salvador

Our food is a mix between corn pork meet and vegetables

Pupusas

A thick handmade corn flour or rice flour flatbread stuffed with cheese and red kidney beans, most common variations (Chicharrón, Loroco, Ajo, Ayote) which are served with salsa roja, a cooked tomato sauce, often served with curtido handmade cabbage.

Canoas

Sweet fried plantains filled with a creamy and delicious thick milk, In El Salvador it is considered as a typical dish that can be enjoyed in the evenings and accompanied by typical drinks like (coffee, Chocolate ).

Pastelitos

Stuffed, fried, succulent and crunchy turnovers, one of the country’s most popular and simplest fast foods, The dough is traditionally seasoned with annatto, which gives it a characteristic orange color, usually stuffed with chicken or pulled beef and vegetables or just with vegetables.

Tamales de pollo

These Tamal de Pollo or Chicken Tamales are a Salvadoran recipe made of savory corn batter with a filling of chicken and vegetables covered in a juicy and delicious Recaudo sauce. Those delicious flavors, aroma, and texture are enhanced and enveloped with green banana or plantain leaves.
